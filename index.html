We are the undeniably beautiful result of an evolutionary fuzzing algorithm running a few billion years on earth. How difficult could it be to use fuzzing and find bugs in the software we, most recently, created ourselves? Especially when we have powerful opetating systems, program analysis, and machine learning? In other words, I am broadly interested in operating systems, program analysis for software testing, and applications of machine learning to system problems.

Research

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Computer Science department of Brown University working under the mentorship of Assistant Professor Vasileios Kemerlis. I received my PhD from Columbia University, under the supervision of Roxana Geambasu. Over my years in Columbia, I collaborated with various great people, including Jason Nieh, Baishakhi Ray, Suman Jana, and Daniel Hsu. Moreover, during the summers of 2017 and 2018, I had two wonderful internships at Microsoft Research, where, along with Marina Polishchuk and Patrice Godefroid, I did seminal work on Cloud Service API Testing.

Before joining Columbia, I was a member of the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN), where I contributed to Agile Infrastructure. Some distinguished former colleagues made these interesting presentations: Configuration Management and CERN's OpenStack Cloud. My alma mater is the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (UOA), Department of Informatics and Telecommunications. I worked there with Alex Delis from Management of Data, Information & Knowledge group and with Mema Roussopoulos from the Distributed Systems Lab.

Most recently, my thoughts that have been revolving around two main questions: how can we automatically test distributed applications, like cloud services, using program analysis and machine learning? what operating system abstractions are necessary to support modern application workloads? I am currently focusing on contributing new ideas (and learning from past ideas) in the areas of program analysis for software testing, operating systems, and applications of machine learning to systems problems. Here is my research statement and my dissertation as well as a literature review in the area of software testing which was part of my candidacy exam.
